# Notations

Notations are a great source of progress. For instance, the rise of standard  music  notations had a profound impact on creativity and growth in Music. Standard domain specific notations also fueled innovation in Electrical, Mechanical Chemical and Civil Engineering. However, excessive reliance on standard notations for business strategy modeling can cause problems. In fact, we think a key reason, possibly the only reason, why most enterprise architecture initiatives fail, is that they tend to treat standard notations as magic bullets that guarantee successful outcomes. Choosing a standard notation that only specialists can understand is a good way to ensure that your models will be developed in isolation, without a clear purpose, and disconnected from the people who will consume and act on them. Standard notations generally offer little benefit in modeling your business, since you are not trying to communicate to the world, only within your organization. To put it bluntly, rigid, complex, one-size-fits-all, standard notations can undermine business strategy modeling efforts. This outcome is quite counterintuitive because it contradicts experience with standard notations in disciplines like Electrical and Civil Engineering. ! 94 business strategy for a multidimensional world However, instead of resisting the facts, why not embrace them? Build “free form” views of your organization, produced “just-in-time” by the people directly involved in devising, documenting and executing the strategy. This proposal strikes most people as unreasonable. However, what most people do not understand initially is that it usually takes far less time to create a custom notation than to learn a standard one with lots of bells and whistles. Also, the results obtained with a custom notation are usually much better than those obtained with a standard notation that was designed for a different purpose and is semantically misaligned with your requirements. Also, standard notations must be complex to meet everyone’s needs, and therefore require large amounts of training to master. It is far better to let people create their own notations and then use them to communicate in small groups. This approach is not new.
